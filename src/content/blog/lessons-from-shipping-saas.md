# Lessons from 6 Years of Shipping SaaS Products

After 6+ years of building SaaS products across healthcare, cybersecurity, and indie projects, here are the patterns that keep showing up.

## 1. Boring Technology Wins

The most successful products I've worked on used the most boring stacks. PostgreSQL, TypeScript, well-tested APIs. The exciting part should be the *product*, not the infrastructure.

## 2. Technical Debt is a Business Decision

At Board of Cyber, I led incremental Angular migrations while shipping features. The key insight: you don't need a "big rewrite." You need a strategy for **continuous modernization**.

## 3. Ship the Ugly Version First

PDFold's first version was embarrassingly basic. But it worked, and real users gave me feedback that no amount of internal testing could have provided.

## 4. Automate Everything That Hurts

If you're doing something manually more than twice, automate it. My work on CI/CD pipelines and the env-doctor CLI tool both came from this principle.

## 5. AI is a Tool, Not a Magic Wand

I use AI pair programming daily. It makes me faster, not lazier. The engineer still needs to understand the system, make architectural decisions, and review every line.
